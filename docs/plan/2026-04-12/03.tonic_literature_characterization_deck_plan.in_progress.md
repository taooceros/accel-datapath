## Design goals

1. Optimize for **self-study clarity**, not for a short spoken update.
2. Rewrite the plan so deck work can proceed **one module at a time**.
3. Keep **one main idea per slide** and require every slide to have a declared takeaway.
4. Make every paper module follow the same teaching shape: **question → mechanism → evidence → implication**.
5. Explicitly list every slide-visible element in the plan before implementation.
6. Prefer diagrams, pipelines, compact result tables, and short interpretation blocks over prose-heavy panels.
7. Keep caveats visible but subordinate to the main takeaway.
8. Preserve traceability for every quantitative claim and named mechanism.
9. Keep RR-Compound explicitly lower-confidence and qualitative unless a later ingestion pass upgrades the evidence.
10. Use the plan itself as the review contract for future deck edits.

## Story spine

1. Open with the decomposition question the literature is supposed to answer.
2. Teach the reader how to use the deck and how each paper will be read.
3. Show how the paper set covers different buckets rather than pretending one paper explains everything.
4. Slow down the strongest papers and teach them in three-step modules: why it matters, how it works, what it found.
5. Use Cornflakes and RR-Compound to refine adjacent design constraints rather than over-weighting them.
6. Reconnect the literature to the real Tonic byte path and repo-local regime evidence.
7. End with characterization rules and the next measurement pass.

Closing message:

> the literature gives the decomposition vocabulary and experimental caution, but the final answer still has to come from a Tonic-specific, bucketed, regime-aware measurement campaign.
